# Equity and Inclusion

This document defines the equity and inclusion commitments of the Open-Source
Cybersecurity Degree and provides practical guidance for delivery partners on
supporting diverse learners.

---

## 1. Commitment

This degree was created on the premise that quality cybersecurity education should
not be gatekept by geography, income, background, or institutional access. That
commitment extends beyond making the curriculum free — it requires actively designing
for the diversity of learners who will use it.

We are committed to:
- Removing financial barriers (the degree is and will remain free)
- Designing content that is accessible to learners with disabilities
- Creating a welcoming environment regardless of background, identity, or prior education
- Not assuming cultural, linguistic, or socioeconomic homogeneity among learners

---

## 2. Financial Access

**Cost:** The degree is free. No payment is required at any point.

**Hardware:** Labs are designed for a minimum spec of 8 GB RAM, quad-core CPU,
50 GB disk — achievable on a second-hand laptop for ~$200–$400 AUD. Where a lab
requires higher spec, this is documented and a lower-resource alternative is provided
where possible.

**Internet:** All resources are accessible on a standard household internet connection.
No large file downloads are required without warning. Lab VM images are documented
with file sizes before download.

**No account walls:** Core learning materials do not require registration, accounts,
or logins. Where tools require accounts (GitHub, etc.), free-tier options are used.

## 3. Disability Access

### Content Accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA)

All content must comply with WCAG 2.1 AA — this is a compliance requirement in
`docs/compliance/program-delivery.md`. Key requirements for contributors:
- All images have descriptive alt text
- Videos have captions (where applicable)
- Heading hierarchy is correct (no skipped levels)
- Colour is not the only indicator of meaning
- Tables have column headers

### Cognitive Load

- Content is written in plain English (CEFR B2 level target)
- Technical terms are defined on first use
- Lab instructions are step-by-step and unambiguous
- Assessment tasks are clearly scoped — students should not have to guess what is required

### Learners with Disabilities in Facilitated Delivery

Delivery partners must:
- Make reasonable adjustments for students who disclose a disability
- Not require disclosure of specific medical or diagnostic information — focus on
  what adjustments are needed, not the cause
- Provide extended time for assessments where requested
- Accept alternative formats for assessment submissions (audio/video in place of
  written, for example) unless the assessment specifically requires written work
- Maintain confidentiality about disability accommodations

## 4. Language and Cultural Inclusion

### English Language

The degree is written in Australian English. Learners for whom English is an
additional language are welcome and valued — the cybersecurity industry in Australia
is linguistically diverse, and this degree should reflect that.

For learners with limited English proficiency:
- Technical terms are defined on first use
- Facilitators should allow additional time for reading and comprehension
- Assessment rubrics do not penalise non-native grammar or spelling in technical
  content — the substance of the technical analysis is what is assessed

**Exception:** SC06 (Stakeholder Communication) and Leadership major units specifically
assess professional communication quality. Learners with EAL backgrounds may need
additional support with these units.

### Cultural Inclusion

- The degree acknowledges the diversity of backgrounds that practitioners bring to
  Australian cybersecurity
- Australian First Nations people are welcomed as learners and contributors
- Content should not assume a particular cultural background — examples and case
  studies should reflect the diversity of Australian organisations and communities
- Australian Indigenous organisations and communities face the same cybersecurity
  threats and regulatory obligations as other Australian entities — this should be
  reflected in content where relevant

### Avoid Jargon and Exclusionary Language

- Do not use military jargon unnecessarily (beyond industry-standard terms)
- Do not use language that implies a default demographic for cybersecurity practitioners
- Do not use competitive framing that excludes beginners ("anyone who doesn't know X
  shouldn't be in this field")

---

## 5. Gender and Diversity

The cybersecurity industry has significant gender diversity challenges. This degree
should not replicate or reinforce them.

**In content:**
- Use gender-neutral language (they/them for unspecified individuals; not "he" as default)
- Include examples and case studies featuring diverse practitioners
- Do not use metaphors or examples that assume a particular demographic

**In community:**
- The code of conduct (see `docs/governance.md`) applies to all interactions
- Harassment, discrimination, or exclusionary behaviour is not tolerated
- Maintainers are responsible for enforcing the code of conduct promptly

**In facilitated delivery:**
- Delivery partners must have a clear process for reporting and responding to
  harassment or discrimination
- Guest practitioners should reflect the diversity of the cybersecurity industry —
  not exclusively presenting the same demographic

## 6. Socioeconomic and Regional Access

### Regional and Remote Learners

The degree is fully online with no physical attendance requirement. This is a
deliberate design choice to serve learners in regional and remote Australia who
do not have access to metropolitan university campuses.

### Career Changers and Non-Traditional Learners

The degree does not require a prior computing background to begin. Foundation
units are designed for learners entering from non-technical backgrounds. Learners
should be honest with themselves about the pace — F01 and F02 may require
additional time for those without prior IT experience.

### Learners Without a Prior University Background

The degree does not require any prior academic qualifications. The learning
outcomes and assessment standards are AQF Level 7 — this is graduate-level work —
but the degree is designed to bring learners to that standard, not to assume they
arrive at it.

---

## 7. Reporting Equity Concerns

If you experience or observe behaviour that undermines the equity and inclusion
commitments in this document:

- **In a facilitated delivery context:** Contact your facilitator or the delivery
  partner's student support contact
- **In the online community (GitHub):** Report via GitHub's built-in reporting tools
  or contact a Maintainer directly
- **Content concerns** (content that is exclusionary, inaccessible, or culturally
  inappropriate): Raise a GitHub Issue with the label `equity-concern`

All reports are treated confidentially.
