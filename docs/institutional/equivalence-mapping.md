# Equivalence Mapping and Credit Recognition

This document maps the Open-Source Cybersecurity Degree to existing Australian
qualifications and documents the pathway for formal credit recognition agreements
with registered education providers.

---

## 1. Purpose

Equivalence mapping serves two audiences:

**Students:** Understand how this degree relates to other qualifications — both
for bringing prior learning in (RPL) and for taking completed work to other institutions.

**Institutions:** Understand the academic equivalence of this degree's units when
considering credit transfer or articulation agreements.

---

## 2. AQF Equivalence

This degree is designed to be equivalent to an AQF Level 7 Bachelor Degree.

| AQF Requirement | This Degree |
|---|---|
| Qualification type | Bachelor Degree (AQF Level 7) |
| Minimum credit points | 144 CP (AQF requirement) | 168 CP |
| Volume of learning | 3 years full-time equivalent | 3 years (self-paced equivalent) |
| Knowledge descriptor | Broad and coherent knowledge in cybersecurity | Covered — see `docs/compliance/aqf-teqsa.md` |
| Skills descriptor | Cognitive and technical skills to analyse and evaluate | Covered — see `docs/compliance/aqf-teqsa.md` |
| Application descriptor | Autonomy, judgement, responsibility | Covered — see `docs/institutional/threshold-learning-outcomes.md` |

**Important caveat:** AQF equivalence is a design intent, not a legal status.
This degree is not issued by a TEQSA-registered provider and does not currently
carry the legal status of an AQF qualification. Formal equivalence can only be
conferred through TEQSA registration or a formal credit recognition agreement
with a registered provider.

## 5. Articulation Agreements

### Current Status

No formal articulation agreements are currently in place. This is a planned
milestone on the recognition pathway (see `docs/accreditation.md`).

### Target Agreement Types

**Credit recognition agreement:** A university agrees to grant a specified number
of credit points to students who present a completed competency profile and portfolio
from this degree. The most achievable near-term form.

**Articulation agreement:** A university agrees to admit students who have completed
specified layers of this degree into a specified year of their program (e.g., admitted
into Year 2 of a cybersecurity bachelor on completion of this degree's Foundation Year).

**Dual-credit arrangement:** A university and this degree agree to recognise each
other's units, so students can study some units from each program.

### How to Approach an Institution

If you are seeking personal credit recognition from a university:
1. Contact the institution's RPL or credit transfer office
2. Provide:
   - Your completed competency profile (`templates/competency-profile-template.md`)
   - Your portfolio of completed lab reports and assessment artefacts
   - Unit outlines from `docs/structure.md`
   - The AQF compliance documentation from `docs/compliance/aqf-teqsa.md`
   - This equivalence mapping document
3. The institution will assess against their own policies

If you are an institution seeking to establish a formal agreement, contact the
project Maintainers via GitHub.
