# AQF Level 7 & TEQSA Compliance

This document defines the compliance requirements for the Open-Source Cybersecurity
Degree against the Australian Qualifications Framework (AQF) Level 7 Bachelor Degree
descriptors and the Tertiary Education Quality and Standards Agency (TEQSA) Higher
Education Standards Framework (HESF).

Every unit must satisfy the AQF Level 7 unit-level checklist. The program as a whole
must satisfy the TEQSA HESF program-level requirements.

---

## 1. AQF Level 7 — Bachelor Degree Descriptor

The AQF Level 7 descriptor defines the knowledge, skills, and application of knowledge
and skills required for a Bachelor degree. All units in this degree must contribute
to meeting these descriptors.

### 1.1 AQF Level 7 Knowledge Descriptor

> *Graduates at this level will have broad and coherent knowledge and understanding in
> one or more disciplines or areas of practice.*

**What this means for unit content:**
- Each unit must cover a coherent body of knowledge within cybersecurity
- Content must go beyond surface-level familiarity to demonstrate depth
- Units must connect their content to the broader discipline and professional context

**Unit compliance checklist:**
- [ ] The unit covers a recognized body of knowledge within its domain (not isolated facts)
- [ ] Content demonstrates both breadth (range of topics) and depth (substantive treatment)
- [ ] Unit explicitly connects its content to real-world cybersecurity practice
- [ ] Content is aligned to current industry and academic knowledge in the domain

---

### 1.2 AQF Level 7 Skills Descriptor

> *Graduates at this level will have well-developed cognitive, technical, and
> communication skills to select and apply methods and technologies to:*
> - *analyse and evaluate information to complete a range of activities*
> - *analyse, generate and transmit solutions to unpredictable and sometimes complex problems*
> - *transmit knowledge, skills and ideas to others*

**What this means for unit content:**
- Units must require students to apply skills, not just recall knowledge
- Labs and assessments must present problems that require analysis and decision-making
- At least one assessment per unit must involve communication of findings or recommendations

**Unit compliance checklist:**
- [ ] Learning outcomes include cognitive skills (analyse, evaluate) appropriate to the unit's layer
- [ ] Labs require students to apply technical skills to realistic scenarios
- [ ] At least one assessment requires students to communicate findings (written or verbal)
- [ ] Assessment tasks include problems without a single prescribed "correct" answer

---

### 1.3 AQF Level 7 Application of Knowledge and Skills Descriptor

> *Graduates at this level will apply knowledge and skills to demonstrate autonomy,
> well-developed judgement, adaptability and responsibility as a practitioner or
> learner in a range of professional contexts.*

**What this means for unit content:**
- Students must be required to make judgements and decisions, not just follow instructions
- Scenarios must reflect professional contexts (not purely academic exercises)
- Australian professional contexts must be included

**Unit compliance checklist:**
- [ ] At least one lab or assessment places students in a realistic professional scenario
- [ ] Australian professional context is explicitly incorporated (legislation, regulators, case studies)
- [ ] Students are required to make judgements or recommendations, not just report facts
- [ ] Assessment rubrics reward professional-quality communication and reasoning

---

### 1.4 AQF Level 7 — Full Unit Compliance Sign-Off

Before a unit can reach "Practitioner Approved" status, the Domain Expert must
confirm compliance with all of the following:

**Knowledge (AQF 7.1):**
- [ ] Unit covers broad and coherent knowledge in its domain
- [ ] Content depth is appropriate for a bachelor-level qualification
- [ ] Content is current and reflects the state of the discipline in 2024+

**Skills (AQF 7.2):**
- [ ] Learning outcomes include cognitive skills at Bloom's levels 3–5 (layer-appropriate)
- [ ] Practical skills are assessed through labs and/or practical assessments
- [ ] Communication skills are assessed through at least one written or verbal task

**Application (AQF 7.3):**
- [ ] Students are required to exercise judgement in professional scenarios
- [ ] Australian professional context is incorporated (legislation, regulators, or case studies)
- [ ] Assessment reflects real-world professional tasks in the domain

**Credit Weighting (8 CP):**
- [ ] Content volume is proportionate to 8 credit points
  - As a guide: 8 CP ≈ 120–160 hours of total student effort
  - Includes: reading/study (≈60–70 hrs), labs (≈30–40 hrs), assessment (≈20–30 hrs)
- [ ] Minimum 6 substantive topics
- [ ] Minimum 2 practical labs
- [ ] Minimum 1 formative + 1 summative assessment

## 2. TEQSA Higher Education Standards Framework (HESF) — Program Level

The TEQSA HESF defines quality standards for higher education providers. While this
project is not yet a registered provider, the degree is designed to align with HESF
from the outset to support a future formal registration pathway.

### Domain 1: Student Participation and Attainment

| HESF Standard | How This Program Addresses It |
|---|---|
| 1.1 — Entry pathways | Open access; no formal entry requirements for community version |
| 1.2 — Student support | Self-directed learning model; community forums; practitioner mentorship pathway (planned) |
| 1.3 — Progression and completion | Modular structure allows flexible progression; units can be completed individually |

### Domain 2: Learning Environment

| HESF Standard | How This Program Addresses It |
|---|---|
| 2.1 — Facilities and infrastructure | Fully online; all required tools are free/OSS; minimum hardware spec documented |
| 2.2 — Accessibility | Content must meet WCAG 2.1 AA; see `docs/compliance/program-delivery.md` |
| 2.3 — Physical safety | N/A (fully online delivery) |

### Domain 3: Teaching

| HESF Standard | How This Program Addresses It |
|---|---|
| 3.1 — Staffing (credentials) | Domain Expert + Practitioner Reviewer sign-off required for all unit content |
| 3.2 — Currency of knowledge | Annual review cycle; trigger-based review for framework updates |
| 3.3 — Course of study design | AQF Level 7 aligned; Bloom's taxonomy; framework-mapped learning outcomes |
| 3.4 — Learning outcomes | 5–8 per unit; mapped to AQF descriptors and workforce frameworks |
| 3.5 — Assessment | Formative + summative; 4-level rubrics; real-world assessment tasks |

### Domain 4: Research and Scholarship

| HESF Standard | How This Program Addresses It |
|---|---|
| 4.1 — Scholarly engagement | Annotated further reading (min 5 per unit); framework currency maintained by Framework Custodian |
| 4.2 — Research literacy | Framework mapping, analytical labs, and capstone project develop research-adjacent skills |

### Domain 5: Institutional Quality Assurance

| HESF Standard | How This Program Addresses It |
|---|---|
| 5.1 — Governance arrangements | Formal governance model documented in `docs/governance.md` |
| 5.2 — Corporate monitoring | GitHub Issues and PRs provide transparent audit trail; versioning policy applied |
| 5.3 — Information for students | README.md and unit metadata provide transparent information on content status |
| 5.4 — Self-assurance | Annual review cycle; practitioner review; community feedback via GitHub Issues |

## 3. Credit Point Compliance

| Component | Credit Points | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| Foundation Year (6 units × 8 CP) | 48 CP | 6 units establishing baseline knowledge and skills |
| Operational/Strategic Core (6 units × 8 CP) | 48 CP | 6 units developing specialised knowledge in chosen pathway |
| Major (6 units × 8 CP) | 48 CP | 6 units developing advanced specialisation |
| Capstone | 24 CP | Integrative project demonstrating synthesis across all layers |
| **Total** | **168 CP** | Within AQF Level 7 requirement of 144–192 CP |

**8 CP per unit — student effort calculation:**

| Activity | Estimated Hours |
|---|---|
| Directed study (topics, readings) | 60–70 hours |
| Laboratory exercises | 30–40 hours |
| Assessment preparation and completion | 20–30 hours |
| **Total per unit** | **~120–140 hours** |

This is consistent with industry norms for 8-credit-point bachelor-level units.

## 4. AQF Learning Outcome Levels — Quick Reference

This table maps the AQF Level 7 knowledge and skills descriptors to Bloom's taxonomy
levels and provides guidance for each degree layer.

| Degree Layer | AQF Emphasis | Bloom's Level | LO Verbs |
|---|---|---|---|
| Foundation Year | Knowledge acquisition + basic application | 1–3 | define, explain, demonstrate |
| Core (Operational/Strategic) | Analysis + applied professional skills | 3–4 | apply, analyse, compare |
| Major Units | Evaluation + professional judgement | 4–5 | evaluate, recommend, assess |
| Capstone | Synthesis + creation of professional artefacts | 5–6 | design, develop, create, synthesise |

The degree is designed with the following recognition pathway in mind:
