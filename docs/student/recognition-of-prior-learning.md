# Recognition of Prior Learning

Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) is the process by which prior study, work
experience, or industry certifications may be recognised as equivalent to one
or more units of this degree.

RPL allows experienced practitioners to enter the degree at a higher level,
skip units they can already demonstrate competency in, or have this degree's
units recognised by other institutions.

---

## 1. Types of Prior Learning

### 1.1 Industry Certifications

The following certifications may be recognised as evidence of competency
equivalent to specific units. Recognition is evidence-based — holding the
certification is not sufficient; you must also demonstrate the competency
applies to the unit's learning outcomes.

| Certification | Potential Unit Equivalence | Evidence Required |
|---|---|---|
| CompTIA Network+ | F01 (Networking Fundamentals) | Certification + demonstration of unit LOs |
| CompTIA Linux+ / LPIC-1 | F02 (OS & Administration) — partial | Certification + lab demonstration |
| CompTIA Security+ | F04 (Security Concepts) — partial | Certification + written assessment |
| GIAC GISF or equivalent | F04 (Security Concepts) | Certification + portfolio evidence |
| GIAC GCIH | OC04 (Incident Response Lifecycle) | Certification + worked example |
| GIAC GCFA or GCFE | DF01–DF03 (DFIR units) — partial | Certification + portfolio evidence |
| GIAC GCTI | CT01–CT03 (CTI units) — partial | Certification + worked example |
| GIAC GDAT or GCED | DE01–DE02 (Detection Engineering) — partial | Certification + artefact |
| OSCP or equivalent | CE01–CE03 (CTE units) — partial | Certification + engagement report |
| CISM | SC03, SC05, LD01–LD02 — partial | Certification + portfolio evidence |
| CISSP | SC01–SC03 — partial | Certification + written assessment |
| ISO 27001 Lead Auditor | GR01–GR03 — partial | Certification + audit artefact |
| IRAP Assessor | GR04–GR06 — partial | Active IRAP status + portfolio |

**Note:** "Partial" recognition means the certification covers some but not all
learning outcomes of that unit. A bridging assessment covering the gap outcomes
is required.

### 1.2 Formal Education (Credit Transfer)

Prior study at an AQF Level 5 (Diploma), Level 6 (Associate Degree), or Level 7
(Bachelor) institution may be recognised for credit.

| Prior Study | Potential Recognition |

Demonstrated professional experience may be recognised for advanced standing in
the degree, particularly for Core and Major units.

| Experience Type | Potential Recognition |

| 5+ years CISO or security manager | SC03–SC06, LD01–LD04 — RPL consideration |

## 2. RPL Assessment Process

### Step 1 — Self-Assessment

Review the learning outcomes for the unit(s) you wish to claim RPL for.
Ask yourself honestly: can I demonstrate each learning outcome with evidence?

Download the unit's framework mapping table and identify which NICE DCWF T-codes
and SFIA skills you can evidence. If you cannot trace your experience to specific
T-codes, full RPL is unlikely to be appropriate.

### Step 2 — Evidence Compilation

Compile your RPL evidence portfolio. For each unit you are claiming:

**For certification-based RPL:**
- Certificate of completion or digital badge (current — not expired)
- A 500–1000 word mapping document explaining how the certification covers each
  learning outcome in the unit
- One artefact (lab report, report excerpt, or equivalent) demonstrating the skill

**For work experience RPL:**
- A statutory declaration or letter from a supervisor confirming your role and
  responsibilities (on letterhead, signed)
- A 500–1000 word mapping document explaining how your experience covers each LO
- At least one work artefact per claimed unit (anonymised/redacted as required by
  your employer's confidentiality requirements)

**For formal study RPL:**
- Official academic transcript
- Unit outline or syllabus of the prior study
- A mapping document explaining which learning outcomes are covered

### Step 3 — Submission

For **self-directed learners**: RPL is self-assessed. You may claim RPL by
documenting your evidence in your competency profile and noting which units
you are claiming rather than completing. There is no external body to submit to.
The value of an honest RPL assessment is the integrity of your portfolio.

For **facilitated delivery (university or employer program)**: Submit your RPL
evidence portfolio to your facilitator or the delivery partner's RPL assessor.
The delivery partner's institutional RPL policy governs the process and timeline.

### Step 4 — Assessment Decision

RPL assessors evaluate whether the evidence demonstrates each learning outcome
at the required Bloom's level for the unit's layer. The decision is:
- **Full RPL granted** — all learning outcomes demonstrated; unit is credited
- **Partial RPL** — some learning outcomes demonstrated; bridging assessment required
- **RPL not granted** — evidence insufficient; complete the unit

---

## 3. What RPL Does Not Cover

RPL cannot substitute for:
- The capstone project (must be completed in full — it is integrative and forward-looking)
- Australian context requirements (even with international certifications, Australian
  regulatory competency must be demonstrated)
- Any unit where the assessment requires producing a current artefact
  (you must demonstrate current, not historical, capability)

## 4. Formal Credit Recognition (Outward)

If you want this degree recognised for credit at a university or TAFE:
1. Complete `docs/institutional/equivalence-mapping.md` — this lists institutions
   that have formal credit recognition agreements
2. Contact the target institution's RPL or credit transfer office with:
   - Your competency profile (`templates/competency-profile-template.md`)
   - Your portfolio of lab reports and assessment artefacts
   - This degree's unit outlines (from `docs/structure.md`)
   - This degree's AQF alignment documentation (`docs/compliance/aqf-teqsa.md`)

Credit transfer is at the discretion of the receiving institution. There are no
automatic credit transfer rights until a formal articulation agreement is in place.
