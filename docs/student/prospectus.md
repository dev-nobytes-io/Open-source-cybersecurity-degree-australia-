# Program Prospectus

## Open-Source Cybersecurity Degree — Australia
### Bachelor of Cybersecurity (Operations) · Bachelor of Cybersecurity (Strategy)
### AQF Level 7 Equivalent · Free · Open-Source · Practitioner-Led

---

## Who Is This For?

This degree is designed for:

- **Career changers** entering cybersecurity from another field, who need structured,
  credible education but cannot afford or access a full university degree
- **IT professionals** with hands-on experience who want to formalise and validate
  their skills against recognised frameworks (NICE DCWF, SFIA 9, ASD)
- **Early-career practitioners** who want a rigorous structured pathway beyond
  scattered certifications
- **Offshore and regional Australians** without convenient access to a metropolitan
  university campus
- **Employers and sponsors** looking for a structured, framework-mapped curriculum
  to develop internal talent
- **Universities and TAFEs** seeking a freely available, accreditation-ready curriculum
  to adopt, adapt, or recognise

## The Two Degrees

### Bachelor of Cybersecurity (Operations)

For practitioners who want to work in hands-on technical roles:
- SOC analysts, threat hunters, incident responders, DFIR specialists
- CTI analysts, detection engineers, red/purple team operators
- Roles mapped to NICE DCWF Protect & Defend, Investigate, and Analyse categories

**Majors available:**
- Threat Hunting
- Digital Forensics & Incident Response (DFIR)
- Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI)
- Detection Engineering
- Cyber Threat Emulation (CTE)

### Bachelor of Cybersecurity (Strategy)

For practitioners moving into architecture, leadership, or governance roles:
- Security architects, GRC professionals, CISOs, security program managers
- Roles mapped to NICE DCWF Oversee & Govern and Securely Provision categories

**Majors available:**
- Security Engineering
- Leadership & CISO
- Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC)

## Degree Structure

### Three-Layer Architecture

```
Layer 1 — Foundation Year (48 CP)
  6 shared units building core technical and conceptual literacy
  All students complete these regardless of pathway

Layer 2 — Degree Core (48 CP)
  6 units deepening knowledge in your chosen pathway
  Operational Core OR Strategic Core

Layer 3 — Major + Capstone (72 CP)
  6 specialist units in your chosen major (48 CP)
  + Integrative capstone project (24 CP)

Total: 168 Credit Points across approximately 3 years
```

### Volume of Learning

Each 8 CP unit is designed for approximately 120–140 hours of student effort:
- Directed study and reading: ~65 hours
- Lab exercises: ~35 hours
- Assessment: ~25 hours

This is consistent with AQF Level 7 credit point norms (1 CP ≈ 15–20 hours).

## What You Will Be Able to Do

### Foundation Graduates

After completing the Foundation Year, students can:
- Configure and analyse network traffic using standard protocols
- Administer Windows and Linux systems at an intermediate level
- Write automation scripts in Python and Bash
- Identify and classify threats using standard security frameworks
- Navigate Australian cybersecurity legislation (Privacy Act, SOCI Act, NDB scheme)
- Query and parse logs using SIEM tooling

### Degree Completers (Operations)

Graduates of the full Operations degree can:
- Conduct hypothesis-driven threat hunting across enterprise environments
- Perform digital forensic investigation to evidential standards under Australian law
- Produce and consume finished cyber threat intelligence using STIX/TAXII and ATT&CK
- Build, test, and deploy detection logic at scale using Sigma and SIEM platforms
- Plan and execute threat emulation exercises aligned to TIBER-AU methodology
- Articulate their capabilities against NICE DCWF work roles with evidence

### Degree Completers (Strategy)

Graduates of the full Strategy degree can:
- Design security architecture using SABSA, Zero Trust, and NIST SP 800-160
- Manage a cybersecurity program aligned to NIST CSF 2.0 and ISO 27001
- Develop and present board-level security risk reporting
- Lead organisational compliance programs under Australian regulatory requirements
- Assess and manage supply chain and vendor risk
- Operate as an information security manager or CISO in an Australian enterprise

## Recognition and Accreditation

### Current Status

This degree is **not currently issued by a TEQSA-registered provider**. It is an
open-source curriculum project.

**What this means:**
- No formal academic transcript or testamur
- No HECS-HELP or government funding eligibility
- No automatic credit recognition at other universities

**What you do have:**
- A practitioner-reviewed, AQF-aligned competency profile
- A framework-mapped portfolio of completed labs and assessments
- A NICE DCWF skills passport you can show employers directly
- Evidence-based competency that exceeds what most formal degrees produce in
  terms of employer-verifiable practical skills

### Pathway to Formal Recognition

See `docs/accreditation.md` and `docs/institutional/external-benchmarking.md`
for the long-term pathway to TEQSA registration and formal recognition.

For credit recognition with existing universities, see
`docs/student/recognition-of-prior-learning.md`.

## Cost

**This degree is free.** All content is published under CC BY 4.0.

You may incur costs for:
- Hardware (if you don't have a suitable laptop) — second-hand options from ~$300 AUD
- Optional industry certifications (GIAC exams are ~$1,000–$2,500 AUD each)
- Optional delivery through a partner institution (fees set by the partner)

The core curriculum, all labs, and all assessment materials are and will remain free.

## How to Get Started

1. Read this prospectus and the student handbook (`docs/student/handbook.md`)
2. Fork the repository to your own GitHub account
3. Copy `templates/competency-profile-template.md` to your fork and fill in your details
4. Begin with unit **F01: Networking Fundamentals**
5. Introduce yourself in the community Discussions tab

There is no enrolment form, no application process, and no waiting list.
Start when you are ready.
