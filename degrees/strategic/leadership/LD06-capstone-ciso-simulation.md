## Overview

The Leadership capstone puts the student in the CISO's chair: they produce a complete
security strategy, roadmap, and risk-prioritised budget for an Australian
organisation in a regulated sector, then present and defend it to a simulated board.
It integrates the whole major — the CISO mandate (LD01), strategy and program
management (LD02), executive risk communication (LD03), team and resourcing (LD04),
and crisis readiness (LD05) — into a single executive deliverable assessed at the
highest cognitive levels: students **create**, **evaluate**, **present**, and
**defend**.

The capstone is the culmination of the major's emphasis on **security program
management and risk**: the deliverable is a board-ready program built on a maturity
assessment, a risk-prioritised roadmap, and an investment case the board can fund.
The scenario must be an Australian organisation in a regulated sector (financial
services, health, or critical infrastructure), grounding the work in real
obligations. LD06 requires LD01–LD05 and the strategic core.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

1. **Evaluate** an Australian organisation's security maturity and risk posture.
2. **Create** a board-ready security strategy, roadmap, and risk-prioritised budget.
3. **Create** an executive presentation that communicates the program in business
   terms.
4. **Evaluate** and defend the strategy under board-level challenge.
5. **Synthesise** the major's disciplines (program, risk, people, communication,
   crisis) into one coherent program.
6. **Evaluate** their own performance and reflect on the CISO role.

## Topics

> The capstone is a supervised, integrative simulation. These "topics" are the
> phases and guidance; the substantive work is the student's own CISO program.

### Topic 1: Scenario and Current-State Assessment

This phase defines the Australian regulated-sector organisation, its risk and
business context, and the success criteria, then assesses current maturity and risk
posture (LD02/SC01/SC05).

**Key concepts:**
- Regulated-sector scenario and context
- Maturity and risk-posture assessment
- Success criteria

---

### Topic 2: Strategy, Roadmap, and Budget

Students build the core deliverable: a risk-prioritised strategy, a multi-year
roadmap, and a budget with a risk-adjusted investment plan (LD02), integrating
people/resourcing (LD04).

**Key concepts:**
- Risk-prioritised strategy and roadmap
- Risk-adjusted budget and investment plan
- Integrating resourcing/people

---

### Topic 3: Risk Narrative and Board Materials

Students craft the board narrative and materials (LD03): the executive story,
dashboard/metrics, and the decision asks — pitched for a non-technical board.

**Key concepts:**
- Executive risk narrative
- Board materials and metrics
- Clear decision asks

---

### Topic 4: Crisis and Resilience Readiness

Students show the program addresses crisis readiness (LD05): notification
preparedness and resilience as part of the strategy, demonstrating the program is
not only preventive.

**Key concepts:**
- Crisis/notification preparedness in the program
- Resilience and recovery posture
- Readiness as a board concern

---

### Topic 5: Presentation and Defence

Students present the program to a simulated board and defend it under questioning —
handling challenge on cost, risk, and trade-offs while maintaining credibility.

**Key concepts:**
- Executive presentation delivery
- Defending under board challenge
- Maintaining credibility and composure

---

### Topic 6: Reflection and the CISO Role

Students evaluate their own performance and reflect on the realities of the CISO
role — what they would do differently and what the simulation revealed about
leading security.

**Key concepts:**
- Honest self-evaluation
- Lessons about the CISO role
- Continued development

## Labs & Exercises

### Lab 1: Strategy & Budget (capstone deliverable)

**Objective:** Produce the board-ready security strategy, roadmap, and risk-
prioritised budget.

**Prerequisites:**
- Topics 1–4

**Environment:**
- No technical environment — an executive deliverable using NIST CSF 2.0 / Essential
  Eight Maturity Model references and `templates/student-portfolio/index.html`. Any
  device within the 8 GB / 4-core / 50 GB spec.

**Instructions:**

1. Confirm the Australian regulated-sector scenario and success criteria with your
   supervisor.
2. Assess current maturity and risk posture; set a target.
3. Build a risk-prioritised 3-year roadmap with a risk-adjusted budget.
4. Integrate resourcing/people and crisis-readiness elements.
5. Map the program to the organisation's regulatory obligations (APRA/SOCI/OAIC as
   applicable).
6. Package the strategy as a board-ready document.

**Expected Output:**

A board-ready security strategy with maturity assessment, risk-prioritised roadmap,
risk-adjusted budget, resourcing, crisis readiness, and regulatory mapping. The
document is suitable for a real board pack.

**Reflection Questions:**

1. Which investment is hardest to justify, and how did you make the case?
2. How does the program balance prevention, detection, and resilience?
3. Where did regulatory obligation drive the strategy?

---

### Lab 2: Board Presentation (simulation activity)

**Objective:** Present and defend the strategy and budget to a simulated board
panel.

**Prerequisites:**
- Topics 5–6 and Lab 1

**Environment:**
- No technical environment — a presentation/simulation, ideally with community
  practitioners (current/former CISOs) playing board roles. Any device.

**Instructions:**

1. Prepare a board presentation (≥15 minutes) leading with business risk and the
   decisions sought.
2. Present the strategy, roadmap, and budget to the simulated board panel.
3. Field board Q&A, defending cost, risk, and trade-off challenges.
4. Adjust/clarify in response to board feedback without losing the core case.
5. Write a post-presentation reflection (performance, lessons, what you'd change).
6. Package the capstone (strategy + presentation + reflection) as a portfolio
   artefact for community practitioner (ideally CISO) review.

**Expected Output:**

A delivered board presentation with Q&A defence, a reflection report, and a
portfolio artefact suitable for employer review. Learners can defend the program
under board-level challenge.

**Reflection Questions:**

1. Which board question most exposed a weakness, and how did you handle it?
2. How well did you balance candour about risk with confidence in the plan?
3. What did the simulation teach you about the CISO role?

## Assessment

### Formative Assessment: Strategy Review Gate

**Type:** Supervisor/peer review of the draft strategy and budget

**Description:** Before the board presentation, the strategy, roadmap, and budget are
reviewed for board-readiness and risk-justification; students revise. A go/no-go
gate into the presentation.

**Learning Outcomes Assessed:** LO1, LO2

**Feedback mechanism:** Structured feedback against a strategy rubric; revision
required before Lab 2.

---

### Summative Assessment: CISO Simulation

**Type:** Executive strategy, board presentation, and reflection

**Description:** The complete simulation: a board-ready security strategy, roadmap,
and risk-prioritised budget for an Australian regulated organisation; a ≥15-minute
board presentation with Q&A defence (community practitioners as board); and a
post-presentation reflection — packaged as a portfolio artefact and reviewed
(ideally by a current/former CISO). Deliverable: strategy document (~3,000–4,000
words) + presentation + reflection.

**Learning Outcomes Assessed:** LO1, LO2, LO3, LO4, LO5, LO6

**Assessment-Learning Outcome Mapping:**

| Assessment Task | Learning Outcomes |
|---|---|
| Strategy, roadmap & budget | LO1, LO2, LO5 |
| Board presentation | LO3 |
| Q&A defence | LO4 |
| Reflection | LO6 |

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary | Proficient | Developing | Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Strategy & program | Coherent, risk-prioritised, board-ready | Sound | Partial | Weak |
| Investment & risk justification | Compelling, risk-adjusted | Reasonable | Thin | Unjustified |
| Presentation | Confident, clear, business-framed | Solid | Mixed | Poor |
| Defence under challenge | Credible, composed, well-reasoned | Adequate | Shaky | Poor |
| Reflection | Insightful, honest, growth-focused | Reasonable | Superficial | Absent |
