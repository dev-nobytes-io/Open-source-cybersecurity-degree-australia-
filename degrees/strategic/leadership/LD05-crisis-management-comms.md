## Overview

When a major incident hits, the security leader's job becomes crisis leadership and
communication. This unit teaches managing a cyber crisis from the top: coordinating
the response at executive level, communicating with staff, customers, media, and
regulators, and meeting Australia's mandatory notification and disclosure
obligations under intense time pressure. It is the highest-stakes communication unit
in the degree, where errors carry legal, financial, and reputational consequences.

The Australian obligations are extensive and time-bound: the **Privacy Act 1988**
NDB scheme (OAIC), **APRA CPS 234** notification timelines, the **SOCI Act**
reporting regime, and **ASX continuous disclosure** for listed entities — plus legal
hold. Because of the real risk of error, the major requires this unit to be reviewed
by someone with genuine Australian breach-notification experience. LD05 builds on
OC04 (Incident Response), SC06, and LD03, and informs the LD06 capstone.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

1. **Evaluate** the security leader's role in coordinating a cyber crisis.
2. **Analyse** the Australian notification and disclosure obligations triggered by a
   crisis and their timelines.
3. **Create** coordinated crisis communications for internal, customer, media, and
   regulator audiences.
4. **Evaluate** media-handling and reputation considerations during a breach.
5. **Analyse** legal-hold and evidence-preservation obligations during a crisis.
6. **Recommend** a crisis-communication plan for an Australian organisation.

## Topics

### Topic 1: Crisis Leadership

This topic teaches leading in a crisis: establishing command and decision-making,
managing tempo and information flow, supporting the response team, and keeping the
executive/board informed without micromanaging the technical response.

**Key concepts:**
- Crisis command and decision-making
- Managing tempo and information flow
- Executive/board engagement during crisis

---

### Topic 2: Australian Notification Obligations

This topic details the obligations and timelines: the NDB scheme (OAIC) eligible-
breach assessment and notification, APRA CPS 234 notification timelines, SOCI
mandatory reporting, and how they run in parallel.

**Key concepts:**
- NDB eligible-breach assessment and notification
- APRA CPS 234 notification timelines
- SOCI reporting; parallel obligations

**Australian context:** This is the core Australian breach-notification regime in
crisis operation.

---

### Topic 3: ASX and Market Disclosure

For listed entities, a cyber crisis can be market-sensitive. This topic covers ASX
continuous-disclosure obligations, materiality assessment for cyber incidents, and
coordinating disclosure with the response and other notifications.

**Key concepts:**
- ASX continuous disclosure and materiality
- Coordinating disclosure with response
- Avoiding selective/incorrect disclosure

**Australian context:** ASX continuous-disclosure obligations for listed entities.

---

### Topic 4: Crisis Communications

This topic teaches producing coordinated communications across audiences —
internal/staff, customers, media, regulators — that are accurate, consistent, timely,
and legally reviewed, avoiding the trust-destroying mistakes seen in real breaches.

**Key concepts:**
- Audience-specific, consistent messaging
- Accuracy, timing, and legal review
- Learning from real breach-communication failures

---

### Topic 5: Media and Reputation

This topic covers media handling and reputation management: the spokesperson role,
holding statements, managing the narrative, and the long reputational tail of a
breach — and how communication choices shape trust and commercial outcomes.

**Key concepts:**
- Spokesperson role and holding statements
- Narrative and reputation management
- The reputational/commercial tail

---

### Topic 6: Legal Hold and Evidence Preservation

This topic covers the leader's obligations around legal hold and evidence: ensuring
preservation (linking to DFIR), privilege considerations, and not compromising
investigation or admissibility through communication or premature action.

**Key concepts:**
- Legal hold and preservation duties
- Privilege considerations
- Protecting investigation and admissibility

**Australian context:** Preservation supports Evidence Act 1995 admissibility (from
DFIR) and regulatory investigations.

## Labs & Exercises

### Lab 1: Notification Set (deliverable activity)

**Objective:** Draft the coordinated regulatory and stakeholder notifications for a
simulated Australian breach.

**Prerequisites:**
- Topics 2, 3, and 6

**Environment:**
- No technical environment — a communication/compliance deliverable using OAIC/APRA/
  ASX references. Any device within the 8 GB / 4-core / 50 GB spec.

**Instructions:**

1. Take a breach scenario at an ASX-listed, APRA-regulated Australian organisation.
2. Assess NDB eligibility and "serious harm"; draft the OAIC and individual
   notifications.
3. Determine APRA notification timing and SOCI reporting (if applicable); note each.
4. Draft an ASX continuous-disclosure statement if the incident is material.
5. Build a timeline showing each obligation's deadline and dependencies.
6. Note the legal-hold/preservation steps that must already be in place.

**Expected Output:**

A coordinated notification set (OAIC, individuals, APRA, SOCI as applicable, ASX)
with a compliance timeline and legal-hold note. Learners can justify each
notification decision and its timing.

**Reflection Questions:**

1. Which obligation had the tightest deadline, and how did it shape sequencing?
2. How did materiality drive the ASX disclosure decision?
3. Where could a communication error create legal exposure?

---

### Lab 2: Crisis Comms Tabletop (simulation activity)

**Objective:** Lead a crisis-communication tabletop, producing coordinated messaging
under evolving conditions.

**Prerequisites:**
- Topics 1, 4, and 5 and Lab 1

**Environment:**
- No technical environment — a facilitated tabletop/simulation. Any device; can be
  run as a group exercise.

**Instructions:**

1. Run the provided breach scenario with timed injects (new facts, media enquiry).
2. As crisis lead, decide command structure and information flow.
3. Produce a staff message, a customer message, and a media holding statement.
4. Keep messaging consistent and accurate as injects change the facts.
5. Coordinate with the (simulated) legal/regulator workstream.
6. Conduct a brief hotwash on what worked and what would change.

**Expected Output:**

A set of coordinated crisis communications produced under evolving conditions, a
command/flow decision, and a hotwash. Learners can show consistency and accuracy
across audiences as facts changed.

**Reflection Questions:**

1. Where did an inject tempt you into an inaccurate or premature statement?
2. How did you keep messages consistent across audiences?
3. What would you change about your command structure next time?

## Assessment

### Formative Assessment: Obligation-Timeline Drill

**Type:** Scenario exercise with answer key

**Description:** Given breach scenarios, students identify which Australian
obligations are triggered and their timelines. Self-marked.

**Learning Outcomes Assessed:** LO2

**Feedback mechanism:** Answer key with the triggered obligations and timelines.

---

### Summative Assessment: Crisis Communication Plan

**Type:** Plan and simulation report

**Description:** For an assigned Australian regulated/listed organisation, students
(a) produce a coordinated notification set (OAIC/APRA/SOCI/ASX as applicable), (b)
draft internal/customer/media communications, (c) analyse legal-hold and disclosure
obligations, and (d) deliver a crisis-communication plan and a reflection on a
tabletop run. Deliverable: 2,500–3,000 word plan with communication artefacts.

**Learning Outcomes Assessed:** LO1, LO2, LO3, LO4, LO5, LO6

**Assessment-Learning Outcome Mapping:**

| Assessment Task | Learning Outcomes |
|---|---|
| Notification set | LO2 |
| Crisis communications | LO1, LO3 |
| Media/reputation & legal hold | LO4, LO5 |
| Crisis-communication plan | LO6 |

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary | Proficient | Developing | Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Notification accuracy | Correct, timely, well-reasoned | Mostly correct | Partial | Incorrect |
| Crisis communication | Coordinated, accurate, consistent | Sound | Inconsistent | Confused |
| Disclosure & legal | Correct materiality & legal-hold handling | Mostly | Superficial | Incorrect |
| Crisis leadership | Decisive, calm, well-structured | Reasonable | Reactive | Poor |
| Communication | Professional, audience-fit | Clear with minor lapses | Disorganised | Unclear |

## Australian Context

This unit incorporates the following Australian context:

- **Privacy Act 1988 NDB scheme (OAIC):** Breach assessment and notification.
- **APRA CPS 234:** Notification timelines for regulated entities.
- **SOCI Act 2018:** Mandatory reporting for critical infrastructure.
- **ASX continuous disclosure:** Materiality and disclosure for listed entities.
- **Australian breaches (Optus, Medibank, Latitude):** Communication lessons.
