## Overview

The GRC capstone integrates the entire major into a complete governance, risk, and
compliance program designed for a defined Australian organisation. Students design
the governance structure, select and implement a risk-management framework, build a
risk register, choose a compliance framework and conduct a gap analysis, map the
organisation's regulatory obligations, and develop an audit and risk-treatment plan —
a portfolio-grade GRC program. It is assessed at the highest cognitive levels:
students **create**, **evaluate**, and **recommend** a full program.

The capstone is the culmination of the GRC major and of the degree's
compliance-as-revenue thesis — a coherent program that manages risk, satisfies
regulators, and unlocks markets, with its maturity expressed via the **OCEG GRC
Capability Model** (see `docs/maturity-models.md`). The scenario must be an Australian
organisation with defined sector and regulatory obligations. GR06 requires GR01–GR05
and the strategic core, and is the **final unit of the degree**.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

1. **Design** a governance structure for an Australian organisation.
2. **Create** a risk-management framework and register for the organisation.
3. **Evaluate** and select a compliance framework and conduct a gap analysis.
4. **Analyse** and map the organisation's regulatory obligations.
5. **Create** an audit plan and risk-treatment plan for the first compliance cycle.
6. **Recommend** a complete, coherent GRC program and assess its maturity.

## Topics

> The capstone is a supervised, integrative design project. These "topics" are the
> phases and guidance; the substantive work is the student's own GRC program.

### Topic 1: Scope and Organisation Context

This phase defines the Australian organisation, its sector and regulatory
obligations, and the success criteria, agreed with the supervisor.

**Key concepts:**
- Organisation, sector, and obligations
- Success criteria
- Scoping the program

---

### Topic 2: Governance Design

Students design the governance structure (GR01): roles, policies, and committee
charters appropriate to the organisation.

**Key concepts:**
- Governance structure and roles
- Policy framework
- Committee charters

---

### Topic 3: Risk Framework and Register

Students select and implement a risk-management framework (GR02) and build a register
(10+ risks, bow-tie for the top 3) with appetite and treatment.

**Key concepts:**
- Risk framework selection
- Register with bow-tie
- Appetite and treatment

---

### Topic 4: Compliance Framework and Gap Analysis

Students select a compliance framework (ISO 27001 or NIST CSF 2.0 + Essential Eight)
and conduct a gap analysis (GR03).

**Key concepts:**
- Framework selection by goal/industry
- Gap analysis
- Crosswalk where useful

---

### Topic 5: Regulatory Mapping and Audit Plan

Students map the organisation's regulatory obligations (GR04: Privacy + at least one
sector-specific obligation) and develop an audit plan for the first compliance cycle
(GR05).

**Key concepts:**
- Regulatory obligation mapping
- Audit plan for the first cycle
- Evidencing approach

**Australian context:** Privacy Act + sector-specific obligation (APRA/SOCI).

---

### Topic 6: Program Integration and Maturity

Students integrate the components into a coherent program with a risk-treatment plan
(priorities, owners), recommend the program, and assess its maturity (OCEG GRC
Capability Model), plus package a portfolio artefact.

**Key concepts:**
- Integrating governance/risk/compliance
- Risk-treatment plan (priorities/owners)
- GRC maturity (OCEG) and portfolio

## Labs & Exercises

### Lab 1: Governance, Risk & Compliance Design (capstone deliverable)

**Objective:** Design the governance, risk, and compliance components of the GRC
program.

**Prerequisites:**
- Topics 1–4

**Environment:**
- No technical environment — a design deliverable using draw.io, spreadsheets, and
  framework references. Any device within the 8 GB / 4-core / 50 GB spec.

**Instructions:**

1. Confirm the Australian organisation, obligations, and success criteria with your
   supervisor.
2. Design the governance structure (roles, policies, committee charters).
3. Select a risk framework and build a register (10+ risks, bow-tie for top 3) with
   appetite.
4. Select a compliance framework and conduct a gap analysis.
5. Produce a crosswalk where it reduces duplication.
6. Ensure the components are internally consistent.

**Expected Output:**

A coherent governance design, a risk framework + register with bow-ties, and a
compliance framework selection with gap analysis — internally consistent. Learners
can justify the framework choices for the organisation.

**Reflection Questions:**

1. How did the organisation's sector drive your framework choices?
2. Where do governance, risk, and compliance reinforce each other?
3. Which gap most threatens the organisation's goals?

---

### Lab 2: Audit & Treatment Plan (capstone deliverable)

**Objective:** Complete the regulatory mapping, audit plan, and risk-treatment plan,
and recommend the integrated program.

**Prerequisites:**
- Topics 5–6 and Lab 1

**Environment:**
- No technical environment — a design/analysis deliverable using
  `templates/student-portfolio/index.html`. Any device.

**Instructions:**

1. Map the organisation's regulatory obligations (Privacy + sector-specific).
2. Develop an audit plan for the first compliance cycle.
3. Produce a risk-treatment plan with priorities and owners.
4. Integrate all components into a coherent GRC program recommendation.
5. Assess the program's maturity (OCEG GRC Capability Model) and target.
6. Package the capstone as a portfolio artefact and submit for community
   practitioner review.

**Expected Output:**

A complete GRC program: regulatory mapping, audit plan, risk-treatment plan, an
integrated program recommendation, a maturity assessment, and a portfolio artefact
suitable for employer review.

**Reflection Questions:**

1. Which regulatory obligation most shaped the program, and how?
2. How does the program turn compliance into commercial value?
3. What is the program's current maturity, and what would raise it?

## Assessment

### Formative Assessment: Program Review Gate

**Type:** Supervisor/peer review of the governance/risk/compliance design

**Description:** Before completing the program, the Lab 1 components are reviewed for
coherence and fit; students revise. A go/no-go gate into the final program.

**Learning Outcomes Assessed:** LO1, LO2, LO3

**Feedback mechanism:** Structured feedback against a GRC-program rubric; revision
required before Lab 2.

---

### Summative Assessment: Complete GRC Program

**Type:** GRC program design document with portfolio artefact

**Description:** The complete deliverable: governance structure; risk framework and
register (10+ risks, bow-tie for top 3); compliance framework selection with gap
analysis; regulatory-obligation mapping (Privacy + sector-specific); audit plan; risk-
treatment plan with priorities/owners; an integrated program recommendation; and a
maturity assessment (OCEG) — packaged as a portfolio artefact and reviewed by a
community practitioner. Deliverable: program document (~4,000–5,000 words) with
register, gap analysis, and plans.

**Learning Outcomes Assessed:** LO1, LO2, LO3, LO4, LO5, LO6

**Assessment-Learning Outcome Mapping:**

| Assessment Task | Learning Outcomes |
|---|---|
| Governance design | LO1 |
| Risk framework & register | LO2 |
| Compliance framework & gap analysis | LO3 |
| Regulatory mapping | LO4 |
| Audit & treatment plan | LO5 |
| Integrated program & maturity | LO6 |

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary | Proficient | Developing | Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Governance & coherence | Integrated, coherent program | Sound | Partial | Disjointed |
| Risk management | Rigorous register + bow-ties + appetite | Adequate | Partial | Weak |
| Compliance & regulatory | Accurate framework + obligation mapping | Mostly | Superficial | Inaccurate |
| Audit & treatment | Practical, owned, prioritised plans | Reasonable | Generic | Weak |
| Maturity & recommendation | Insightful OCEG maturity; compelling recommendation | Reasonable | Thin | Absent |
