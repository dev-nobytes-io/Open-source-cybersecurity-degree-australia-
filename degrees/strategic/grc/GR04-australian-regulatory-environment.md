## Overview

This unit is the degree's deep dive into the Australian cybersecurity regulatory
environment — the obligations a GRC practitioner must navigate daily. It covers the
**Privacy Act 1988** and the **Notifiable Data Breaches (NDB)** scheme, **APRA CPS
234** (and the operational-risk standard CPS 230), the **Security of Critical
Infrastructure Act 2018 (SOCI)**, and the role and enforcement posture of regulators
(**OAIC**, **APRA**, **ASD/ACSC**, **ASIC**). Students learn not just what the
obligations are, but how to determine which apply, what they require, and how to
evidence compliance.

GR04 is entirely Australian-context and is the unit most likely to contain errors
without expert review — its sign-off requires someone with direct knowledge of the AU
regulatory environment. It builds on F05 (Legal/Ethics) and GR01–GR03, and is central
to the GR06 capstone. Privacy reform is ongoing, so currency must be checked at
review.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this unit, students will be able to:

1. **Analyse** the Privacy Act 1988 and Australian Privacy Principles as they apply
   to an organisation.
2. **Apply** the NDB scheme to determine and execute breach-notification obligations.
3. **Evaluate** APRA CPS 234 (and CPS 230) requirements for a regulated entity.
4. **Analyse** the SOCI Act obligations for a critical-infrastructure entity.
5. **Evaluate** the roles and enforcement posture of Australian regulators.
6. **Recommend** a regulatory-compliance approach for a defined Australian
   organisation.

## Topics

### Topic 1: The Privacy Act 1988 and the APPs

This topic covers the Privacy Act and the 13 Australian Privacy Principles in depth:
personal vs sensitive information, the security principle (APP 11), cross-border
disclosure, access/correction, and current reform directions.

**Key concepts:**
- The 13 APPs, with a security focus (APP 11)
- Personal vs sensitive information
- Reform directions (check currency)

**Australian context:** The core Australian data-protection regime.

---

### Topic 2: The Notifiable Data Breaches Scheme

This topic teaches the NDB scheme operationally: eligible-breach assessment, the
"serious harm" test, the 30-day assessment obligation, notification content, and the
practitioner's role — connecting to incident response (OC04/DFIR).

**Key concepts:**
- Eligible breach and "serious harm"
- 30-day assessment and notification content
- The GRC role in NDB compliance

**Australian context:** Administered by the OAIC; central to AU breach response.

---

### Topic 3: APRA CPS 234 and CPS 230

This topic covers the prudential standards: CPS 234 information-security requirements
(capability, controls, testing, notification) and the broader operational-risk and
service-provider standard CPS 230 — and what they require of a regulated entity.

**Key concepts:**
- CPS 234 requirements and notification
- CPS 230 operational risk / service providers
- Evidencing prudential compliance

**Australian context:** The prudential regime for APRA-regulated entities.

---

### Topic 4: The SOCI Act 2018

This topic covers the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act: covered sectors and
responsible entities, the risk-management program obligation, mandatory cyber-incident
reporting to the ACSC, and enhanced obligations for systems of national significance.

**Key concepts:**
- Covered sectors and responsible entities
- Risk-management program obligation
- Mandatory ACSC incident reporting

**Australian context:** The critical-infrastructure regime reshaping AU obligations.

---

### Topic 5: Regulators and Enforcement

This topic covers the regulators and how they act: OAIC (privacy/NDB), APRA
(prudential), ASD/ACSC (national cyber, SOCI reporting), and ASIC (corporate/financial
cyber expectations), with enforcement examples and what they signal.

**Key concepts:**
- OAIC, APRA, ASD/ACSC, ASIC roles
- Enforcement posture and examples
- What enforcement signals for compliance

**Australian context:** The Australian regulator landscape and enforcement.

---

### Topic 6: Determining and Evidencing Obligations

This topic teaches the practical skill of determining which obligations apply to a
given organisation (by sector, data, size, listing status) and how to evidence
compliance to each regulator — the working judgement of an AU GRC practitioner.

**Key concepts:**
- Determining applicable obligations
- Overlapping/parallel obligations
- Evidencing compliance to regulators

## Labs & Exercises

### Lab 1: NDB Notification (deliverable activity)

**Objective:** Determine whether a fictional breach triggers NDB notification and
draft the OAIC notification.

**Prerequisites:**
- Topics 1–2

**Environment:**
- No technical environment — a compliance deliverable using OAIC NDB guidance (free).
  Any device within the 8 GB / 4-core / 50 GB spec.

**Instructions:**

1. Read the provided fictional breach scenario.
2. Assess whether it is an eligible data breach likely to result in serious harm,
   documenting your reasoning against OAIC criteria.
3. Determine the notification obligation and timeframe.
4. Draft the statement to the OAIC and to affected individuals (required content).
5. Note any parallel obligations (APRA/SOCI) if the entity is in scope.
6. Identify how earlier IR (OC04) would have eased compliance.

**Expected Output:**

A documented NDB eligibility assessment, a draft OAIC/individual notification meeting
required content, and a note on parallel obligations. Learners can justify the
notify/not-notify decision against OAIC criteria.

**Reflection Questions:**

1. What most influenced the "serious harm" determination?
2. How do NDB and any parallel APRA/SOCI obligations interact?
3. How does GRC support, not replace, incident response here?

---

### Lab 2: APRA CPS 234 Mapping (analysis activity)

**Objective:** Map a fictional financial institution's controls against APRA CPS 234
requirements.

**Prerequisites:**
- Topics 3, 5, and 6

**Environment:**
- No technical environment — an analysis deliverable using APRA CPS 234/CPG 234
  references. Any device.

**Instructions:**

1. Take a fictional APRA-regulated financial institution with a described control
   environment.
2. Map its controls against the CPS 234 requirements (capability, controls, testing,
   notification, third parties).
3. Identify gaps and the regulatory risk each creates.
4. Note where CPS 230 service-provider obligations also apply.
5. Recommend a prioritised remediation path.
6. Determine the CPS 234 notification obligations for a hypothetical incident.

**Expected Output:**

A CPS 234 control mapping with gaps, CPS 230 considerations, a remediation path, and
a notification determination. Learners can defend the gap analysis against the
standard.

**Reflection Questions:**

1. Which CPS 234 requirement is hardest to evidence, and why?
2. How do CPS 234 and CPS 230 interact for service providers?
3. What would APRA expect to see as evidence of compliance?

## Assessment

### Formative Assessment: Obligation-Trigger Drill

**Type:** Scenario exercise with answer key

**Description:** Given organisation/breach scenarios, students determine which AU
obligations apply (Privacy/NDB, APRA, SOCI) and the timelines. Self-marked.

**Learning Outcomes Assessed:** LO2, LO4

**Feedback mechanism:** Answer key with the triggered obligations and timelines.

---

### Summative Assessment: Regulatory Compliance Analysis

**Type:** Analytical report

**Description:** For an assigned Australian organisation, students (a) determine all
applicable regulatory obligations, (b) produce an NDB assessment and notification for
a given breach, (c) map controls to APRA CPS 234 (or SOCI obligations) with gaps, and
(d) recommend a regulatory-compliance approach with an evidencing strategy.
Deliverable: 2,500–3,000 word report.

**Learning Outcomes Assessed:** LO1, LO2, LO3, LO4, LO5, LO6

**Assessment-Learning Outcome Mapping:**

| Assessment Task | Learning Outcomes |
|---|---|
| Applicable-obligation determination | LO5, LO6 |
| NDB assessment & notification | LO1, LO2 |
| APRA/SOCI mapping | LO3, LO4 |
| Compliance approach | LO6 |

**Rubric:**

| Criterion | Exemplary | Proficient | Developing | Beginning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Regulatory accuracy | Precise, current AU obligations | Mostly correct | Partial | Incorrect |
| Notification analysis | Correct, well-reasoned | Mostly | Partial | Incorrect |
| Mapping & gaps | Rigorous, evidence-aware | Sound | Superficial | Weak |
| Compliance approach | Practical, evidencing-focused | Reasonable | Generic | Absent |
| Communication | Clear, professional | Clear with minor lapses | Disorganised | Unclear |
